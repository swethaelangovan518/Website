Editorial Policy

ICSAI 2026 follows a rigorous peer-review and publication ethics policy to ensure originality, quality, transparency, and academic integrity.

Publication Standards

Publication Ethics & Review Policy

High-quality peer review standards for international academic publications

📚

Editorial Review Process

All manuscripts submitted to ICSAI 2026 undergo a strict editorial and peer-review process conducted by international experts. Papers are evaluated for originality, technical quality, innovation, readability, and relevance to the conference scope.

Step 01

Initial Submission Check

All submitted papers are checked for formatting, plagiarism, originality, and conference scope compatibility.

Step 02

Plagiarism Screening

Manuscripts undergo plagiarism detection using professional similarity tools. High-similarity papers are rejected immediately.

Step 03

Double-Blind Peer Review

Qualified submissions are assigned to at least two independent reviewers for a double-blind review process.

Step 04

Reviewer Evaluation

Reviewers evaluate papers on originality, technical contribution, clarity, methodology, references, and scientific significance.

Step 05

Revision & Author Response

Authors may be requested to revise their manuscripts according to reviewer comments and submit detailed responses.

Step 06

Final Decision

The editorial committee makes the final decision regarding acceptance, rejection, or further revision.

📝

Originality Policy

Submitted papers must contain original and unpublished research. Manuscripts currently under review elsewhere are not permitted.

⚖️

Publication Ethics

ICSAI 2026 strictly follows international publication ethics standards and does not tolerate plagiarism, data fabrication, or duplicate submission.

👤

Author Responsibility

Authors are fully responsible for the accuracy, authenticity, and ethical integrity of their submitted work.

⚠️

Compliance Notice

Any violation of publication ethics — including plagiarism, data fabrication, authorship misrepresentation, or simultaneous submission — will result in immediate rejection and may be reported to the author's institution. ICSAI 2026 reserves the right to retract published papers if misconduct is discovered post-publication.
