⚖️ Ethics & Responsible AI Usage

AI Tool Guidelines

ICSAI 2026 promotes responsible, ethical, and transparent usage of Artificial Intelligence tools in academic writing and research.

Overview

Responsible AI Usage

ICSAI 2026 recognizes the increasing role of Artificial Intelligence tools in academic research, writing assistance, language enhancement, and data analysis. While these tools can support the research process, their use must remain within clear ethical and academic integrity boundaries.

Authors bear full responsibility for the accuracy, originality, validity, ethics, and integrity of all submitted work. AI systems cannot be listed as authors or co-authors of any submitted paper under any circumstances.

Standards

AI Tool Policy

🤖

Permitted Usage

Authors may use AI tools for grammar correction, language refinement, coding support, and manuscript readability improvement, provided the core intellectual contribution remains the author's own.

✍️

Human Responsibility

Authors are fully responsible for verifying all AI-generated outputs and research content. Errors or inaccuracies introduced by AI tools remain the author's responsibility.

🚫

No AI Authorship

AI tools cannot be credited as authors because they cannot assume ethical, legal, or academic accountability for the submitted work.

📋

Transparency & Disclosure

Authors must disclose any significant use of AI writing or analysis tools within the manuscript's acknowledgements or methods section.

🔍

Originality Requirement

Papers generated entirely by AI without meaningful human intellectual contribution are strictly prohibited and will be desk-rejected.

⚖️

Ethical Compliance

All submissions must comply with research ethics, applicable copyright laws, data privacy regulations, and anti-plagiarism standards.

Compliance

Important Notice

📌

All Authors Must Be Aware of the Following

All submitted papers will undergo thorough plagiarism and AI-content screening before the peer-review process begins.
Any misuse of AI-generated content, fabricated data, or undisclosed AI assistance may result in immediate rejection and potential reporting to the author's institution.
The conference programme committee reserves the right to request clarification or additional documentation regarding AI tool usage at any stage of review.
Authors must ensure that any AI tools used do not violate intellectual property rights, privacy regulations, or third-party data agreements.
